### **IELTS Writing Task 2:**

**Globalisation has accelerated due to advances in technology and international trade, impacting cultures and economies worldwide. To what extent do you agree or disagree?**

Globalization is not a recent phenomenon exclusive to the 21st century; rather, it has been unfolding for centuries, beginning with early trade expeditions led by Western explorers to Asia, the Americas, and beyond. As these explorers reached new territories, complex transformations in societies and cultures ensued, resulting in both conflicts and significant economic benefits for international corporations and local leaders alike. Today, globalization extends far beyond the mere exchange of goods; it encompasses diverse domains such as education, collaborative research in medicine, energy projects, construction contracts, and even arms trade. 

The unprecedented advancements in technology have compelled countries to form alliances to sustain their economies. For instance, a resource-rich but technologically limited country may trade natural resources like lithium with a wealthier nation possessing advanced technology capable of manufacturing tangible products such as lithium batteries or nanochips. Similarly, agriculturally abundant countries can exchange produce with nations facing climatic challenges that limit cultivation. This interconnected system functions like a vast machine, where the failure of a single component can disrupt the entire global supply chain. 

However, globalization also poses challenges, notably the erosion of cultural diversity. Historically, people in China, Korea, America, and Western countries exhibited distinct clothing styles, architectural designs, wedding traditions, and other cultural practices. Yet, due to prolonged exposure to global influences, these unique cultural identities have increasingly converged. Recognizing both the positive and negative impacts of globalization is crucial for young people. They should leverage the benefits provided by modern necessities such as online learning platforms and global networking opportunities to advance their careers, while simultaneously preserving their cultural identities and contributing their talents to their nations' development.
