**Art classes, such as painting and drawing, are as important for children’s development as other subjects, so they should be compulsory in high school. Do you agree or disagree?***
Arts and creative subjects such as painting and drawing play a meaningful role in children's development. However, whether they should be compulsory at high school level remains debatable. This essay disagrees that art should be mandatory in high school, while acknowledging its value when introduced to younger children.

One key reason against making art compulsory in high school is that not all students possess the same aptitude or passion for it.  Forcing high school students to study art against their will may produce the opposite of the intended effect. Students who have had little prior exposure to art or who have not developed a passion for it may find themselves struggling alongside peers who have nurtured these skills from an early age. Rather than fostering creativity, this could create frustration and disengagement.

Furthermore, unlike subjects such as mathematics or literature, which underpin logical thinking and communication skills applicable across virtually all professions, drawing and painting primarily serve students pursuing careers in fields such as graphic design or architecture. Mandating a subject with such limited occupational relevance may disadvantage students who would benefit more from dedicating that time to subjects aligned with their strengths and future goals.

That said, introducing art to younger children is something this essay strongly advocates. By exposing children to art at an early age, educators help them develop the ability to form and express their own opinions through observation. Since artistic interpretation is largely subjective, encouraging children to voice their perspectives trains critical thinking — a skill that is increasingly rare yet universally valuable in modern life.

In conclusion, while art education is undeniably beneficial when introduced early, it should remain an optional subject at high school level. This allows students to prioritise foundational skills essential for their future independence, while those with a genuine passion for art are still free to pursue it.
